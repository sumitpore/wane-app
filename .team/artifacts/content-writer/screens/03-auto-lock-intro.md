## Permission Requests

When the user taps "Start your first water," the app requests necessary permissions one at a time. Each permission is requested only at this point or when the relevant feature is first triggered.

### Accessibility Service Permission

| Element | Copy |
|---------|------|
| **Explanation (shown before system dialog)** | Wane needs this to keep other apps quiet while your water is running. |
| **If denied** | You can turn this on later in Settings. Some features won't work without it. |

### Notification Access Permission

| Element | Copy |
|---------|------|
| **Explanation (shown before system dialog)** | This lets Wane keep notifications quiet during your water. Calls and texts from your emergency contacts will always come through. |
| **If denied** | Notifications from other apps may still appear during your water. You can change this in Settings. |

### Do Not Disturb Permission

| Element | Copy |
|---------|------|
| **Explanation (shown before system dialog)** | This lets Wane silence your phone while the water is running. |
| **If denied** | Your phone may still vibrate or ring during your water. You can change this in Settings. |

## Notes
- "One more thing." is conversational, not Apple-keynote dramatic. The period keeps it grounded.
- "Start your first water" — the water is the thing, not the session.
- Permission explanations use "your water" instead of technical terms.
- Frame everything as what the app does, not what it prevents. Say "keep quiet" not the b-word.
- If a permission is denied, the language is factual, not guilt-laden. No "The app won't work properly!"
